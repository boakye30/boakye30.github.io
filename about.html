Welcome Message

A Message from Our Leadership

Welcome to St. Theresa Hospital

For over fifty years, St. Theresa Catholic Hospital has been part of the fabric of Nkoranza. We have walked alongside families through their most vulnerable moments — births, surgeries, recoveries, and loss — and that privilege is not something we take lightly.

Healthcare here is not just a service. It is a calling. Every member of our staff — from the doctors and nurses to the porters and pharmacists — understands that the person in front of them is someone's mother, father, child, or friend. That understanding shapes everything we do.

We are not a perfect institution, but we are a committed one. We keep investing in people, in equipment, and in the trust of the communities we serve. That work continues every single day.

We are glad you are here, and we are ready to help.

Our Journey

History About The Hospital

St. Theresa Hospital opened its doors in 1974, born out of a simple but urgent reality: the people of Nkoranza needed care, and there was almost nowhere to get it. The Catholic Church stepped in, and what began as a small clinic with limited supplies grew — slowly, stubbornly — into something the region could depend on.

The early years were not easy. Staff worked with whatever was available, often improvising where resources fell short. What they could not improvise was the commitment. Doctors, nurses, and community volunteers showed up day after day, and the hospital held.

Key Milestones:

Decade by decade, the hospital expanded its reach. New wards were added, specialist services brought in, and partnerships formed with health institutions that shared the same values. Today, St. Theresa offers everything from maternity care to neurosurgery — services that most communities in the region still cannot access anywhere else.

Fifty years on, the name and the mission are unchanged. The building is bigger, the equipment more advanced, but the reason we are here remains exactly the same.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

God Fearing

Faith is not a backdrop here — it is the reason this hospital exists. We approach every patient and every decision with humility, knowing that our work is ultimately a form of service to something greater than ourselves.

Respectfulness

Every person who comes through our doors deserves to be treated with dignity — full stop. Their background, their beliefs, their circumstances: none of that changes the quality of care and attention they receive from us.

Teamwork

Good healthcare is never one person's achievement. It takes doctors, nurses, cleaners, administrators, and community workers all pulling in the same direction. We build that culture deliberately and protect it.

Honesty

We tell people the truth — about diagnoses, about limitations, about what we can and cannot do. Trust is hard-earned and easily lost, and we treat it accordingly.

Professionalism

Standards matter. We hold ourselves to high clinical and ethical benchmarks not because we are required to, but because the people we serve deserve nothing less. That means continuous training, honest self-assessment, and accountability at every level.

Discipline

Consistency is what turns good intentions into good outcomes. We follow our protocols, we show up prepared, and we hold each other accountable — because that discipline is what keeps patients safe.
